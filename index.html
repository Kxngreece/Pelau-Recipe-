Trinidadian Pelau Recipe

Pronounced: PAY-LAO.
Trinidadian Pelau is a popular one-pot dish in Trinidad and Tobago that combines meat, rice, and pigeon peas with Caribbean flavors. It is a delicious and flavorful dish that is perfect for family gatherings, parties, or any occasion.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups of parboiled rice
  • 2 lb of chicken (cut into pieces for stew)
  • 3 tablespoons green seasoning
  • 1 can of coconut milk
  • 2 cans of pigeon peas
  • 1/4 cup of brown sugar
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup chopped pimento pepper (flavor pepper)NOT JAMAICAN PIMENTO
  • 2 tablespoons chopped shadow beni (culantro)
  • 2 tablespoons chopped parsley
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1/2 teaspoon of thyme
  • 1/2 cup cubed pumpkin
  • 2 tablespoons chopped ginger
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
Instructions
  1. Wash and season the chicken with salt,pepper, green seasoning . (Let it marinate for at least 1 hour.)
  2. Heat a large pot over medium heat add oil and the brown sugar(when oil is hot). Allow it to caramelize until it becomes a dark brown syrup.
  3. Add the seasoned chicken to the pot and cook until browned on all sides.
  4. Stir in the chopped onion, garlic, and carrot. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add the coconut milk, pigeon peas, and rice to the pot. Mix well.
  6. Pour in enough water to cover the ingredients. Stir and bring to a boil.
  7. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer until the rice is fully cooked and the liquid is absorbed. (This usually takes about 25-30 minutes.)
  8. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking and ensure even cooking.

Serving Suggestions

Serve your delicious Trinidadian Pelau with a side of coleslaw or sliced avocado.
